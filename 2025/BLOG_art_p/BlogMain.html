Depeche Mode

Depeche Mode (DM) is an English electronic band formed in 1980 in Basildon. The core members include Dave Gahan and Martin Gore, with Andy Fletcher until his passing in 2022. Starting with synthpop, they evolved into a darker, more experimental sound. Albums like "Violator" (1990) and hits like "Enjoy the Silence" made them global icons. Known for their emotional depth and innovation, Depeche Mode remains one of the most influential electronic bands of all time.

Michigan

Michigan is a Swedish synthpop and electronic band formed in the early 2000s by Jonas Karlsson and Magnus Löfdahl. Their music blends melodic synth lines, emotional vocals, and polished production, drawing from classic synthpop influences with a modern touch. Their debut, "Graceful and in Sin" (2002), and follow-up, "Ultimate Sky" (2004), received praise within the electronic and EBM communities. While not mainstream, Michigan has built a dedicated fanbase in the European synth scene.

ABBA

ABBA were a Swedish pop group formed in Stockholm in 1972. They are one of the most popular and successful musical groups of all time, and are one of the best-selling music acts in the history of popular music. In 1974, ABBA became Sweden's first winner of the Eurovision Song Contest with the song "Waterloo". During the band's main active years, it consisted of two couples: Fältskog and Ulvaeus, and Lyngstad and Andersson. With the increase of their popularity, their personal lives suffered, which eventually resulted in the collapse of both marriages. The relationship changes were reflected in the group's music, with later songs featuring darker and more introspective lyrics.

Joost Klein

Joost Klein is a Dutch musician, rapper, singer, and former YouTuber. Mainly a hip hop artist. He has released seven studio albums, two of which reached the top ten of the Dutch Album Top 100. "Europapa" was selected to represent the Netherlands in the Eurovision Song Contest 2024 and subsequently became Klein's first song to top both the Dutch Top 40 and Single Top 100. Having qualified in second place from the second semi-final, he was scheduled to perform fifth in the final, but was disqualified from the competition due to allegations of misconduct. These were investigated by Swedish prosecutors, who dropped the case in August 2024 after concluding that they could not prove any criminal conduct.

Muse

Muse are an English rock band from Teignmouth, Devon, formed in 1994. Muse have won numerous awards, including two Grammy Awards, two Brit Awards, five MTV Europe Music Awards and eight NME Awards. In 2012, they received the Ivor Novello Award for International Achievement from the British Academy of Songwriters, Composers and Authors. As of October 2022, they had sold more than 30 million albums worldwide with their most popular songs "Uprising" and "Supermassive black hole"

Royksopp

Röyksopp are a Norwegian electronic music duo from Tromsø formed in 1998. The duo consists of childhood friends who formed Röyksopp. After experimenting with different genres of electronic music, the band solidified their place in the electronica scene with their 2001 debut album, Melody A.M., released on the Wall of Sound record label. Röyksopp has consistently experimented with various genres pertaining to electronic music. Stylistically, the band makes use of various genres like synth-pop.

De/vision

De/Vision is a German synth-pop musical group formed in 1988. The band started as a quartet. One of them left the band in 1991 and other departed in 2000. The name De/Vision is a play on words.The majority of the band's lyrics are in English, but they have had some songs in German. The band is currently signed to Metropolis Records in North America. Roxette

Roxette is a Swedish pop rock duo originally consisting of Marie Fredriksson and Per Gessle, both of whom were already established musicians in Sweden prior to the band's formation. Fredriksson had released a number of successful solo albums, while Gessle had been the lead singer Their international breakthrough came with the release of Look Sharp! in 1988. The album contained two Billboard Hot 100 number ones: "The Look" and "Listen to Your Heart". They performed to over 1.5 million people during the "Join the Joyride! Tour".

Queens of The Stone Age.

Queens of the Stone Age is a rock band formed in 1996 by Josh Homme after the breakup of Kyuss. Blending stoner rock, alternative, and hard rock, they're known for heavy riffs and hypnotic grooves. They rose to fame with "Rated R" (2000) and "Songs for the Deaf" (2002), featuring Dave Grohl. Despite lineup changes, Homme remains the driving force. QOTSA is praised for their unique sound and powerful live shows, with albums like "...Like Clockwork" and "In Times New Roman..." showcasing their lasting influence..
