What is Windows Vista?

Windows Vista is a Microsoft operation system released in 2006, it was a direct successor of Windows XP, and a Windows Longhorn revamp on the default NT base of Microsoft Windows operation systems. Although Longhorn as an operating system was completely scrapped, "Longhorn" was still the codename of Vista until its eventual release.

Nowadays Vista is used by a couple percent of total PC users, which is really decent considering the fact that Vista is sometimes considered as a failure, and was made in 2006. People love it mostly for the atmosphere, the Windows Aero style of operating system is loved not only by Vista users, but users of other "Aero" OS-s such as Windows 7 (the main Vista successor) as well.

Optimization

Windows Vista was coming with the default pack of Microsoft Windows bundled software (firmware) such as Internet explorer, or games like minesweeper. Although Vista was supposed to be a sleek and modern operational system, it was plagued with severe lags, especially on low-end devices. The lag was caused not only by the apps, but also by the aero theme of the OS. Despite the fact that it was the year 2006, most Vista builds required hardware that could run Windows 11 nowadays.

Despite the fact that Microsoft released an OS that was not exactly what people expected such a company to release, they still needed to break even. After all, Longhorn took way too long to be developed. This is why Vista was coming in multiple versions, mostly unnescesarry in usual situations:

Vista Starter "Starter" was coming with severe limitations for usage. It disallowed the usage of many features such as computer-to-computer sharing via a home network, it only allowed 3 programs to be opened at the same time, and had a RAM limitation of 1 GB. There were many more limitations, but the main reason of them being there is the OS price. Out of all Vista versions it is the cheapest, costing up to $20.
Vista Home Basic "Home" is the casual variation for all Windows OSs nowadays. "Home Basic" version of Vista was the definition of a casual-use OS. No BitLocker, no advanced protection etc, but it did have the Aero theme included, it had games and no limit of programs opened at once. Basically everything that a usual person would need.
Vista Home Premium This version brought multimedia extensions: DVD maker, DVD burner, "Windows Meeting Space", XBox 360 support with Media Center, etc. This version also allowed people to have up to 16 GB of RAM.
Vista Business As it says in the title, this Vista was created to suit businessess. No games, but a backup restoration, no XBox integration but additional virus protection. It could also support up to 128 GB of RAM and 2 physical CPUs.
Vista Enterprise "Enterprise" allowed for legal use of virtual machines, and included a multilingual user interface. It also came with a hard drive encryptor.
Vista Ultimate This beast of a Vista costed up to $400 and included EVERYTHING from EVERY Vista variation, without any limitations. Funny how I downloaded it for free 18 years after it was released.
